Troubleshooting Guides

Localised guides for Ugandan devices. Find your brand and fix it yourself.

1. Go to Settings → Battery and enable Battery Saver mode.

2. Disable Background App Refresh for apps you don't use often.

3. Reduce screen brightness to below 50% — the screen uses the most power.

4. Turn off Location Services when not needed: Settings → Location → Off.

5. Check for apps with high battery usage under Settings → Battery → Battery Usage.

💡 If battery still drains in under 4 hours, the cell may need replacing. Submit a repair request.

1. Hold the Power button for 15 seconds — this forces a hard reboot.

2. Connect to charger for 30 minutes before trying again.

3. Try a different charger and cable — faulty cables are a common cause.

4. If the screen flickers but doesn't boot, hold Power + Volume Down to enter recovery mode.

5. In recovery, select Wipe Cache Partition and reboot.

⚠ If none of these work, the motherboard or screen may be faulty. Don't attempt disassembly — contact our engineers.

1. Clear cached data: Settings → Storage → Cached Data → Clear Cache.

2. Uninstall apps you haven't opened in 30+ days.

3. Go to Developer Options → reduce Animation Scale to 0.5x for snappier feel.

4. Move photos and videos to an SD card or Google Photos to free up RAM.

5. Restart the phone at least once a week.

💡 If still slow after clearing cache, a factory reset often restores performance. Back up data first.

1. Forget the network: Wi-Fi → Long press network → Forget. Then reconnect.

2. Restart your router by unplugging it for 30 seconds.

3. Go to Settings → Reset Network Settings.

4. Check if other devices connect — if not, the issue is the router, not the phone.

5. Ensure the phone's date and time are correct — this can prevent Wi-Fi authentication.

1. If the screen is cracked but functional, apply a tempered glass protector immediately to prevent further damage.

2. If display shows lines or black patches, avoid pressing on the screen — this worsens LCD damage.

3. Connect to a TV or monitor via USB-C to HDMI to back up your data if screen is unresponsive.

4. Screen replacement costs approximately UGX 80,000–250,000 depending on model.

ℹ Screen repairs require physical servicing. Book a repair appointment.

1. Open Samsung Device Care → Security → Scan Device.

2. Uninstall any apps you didn't intentionally install, especially from outside Play Store.

3. Go to Settings → Apps → look for apps with generic icons and no description.

4. Clear browser cache: Chrome → Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data.

5. Enable Google Play Protect: Play Store → Profile → Play Protect → Scan.

⚠ Never install APKs from unknown WhatsApp forwards. This is the #1 malware source in Uganda.

1. Place laptop on a hard flat surface — never on beds or cushions that block vents.

2. Use compressed air to blow out dust from the vents every 6 months.

3. Open Task Manager (Ctrl+Shift+Esc) → check for processes using 90%+ CPU and end them.

4. Update your BIOS and drivers from the manufacturer's website.

5. Set Power Plan to Balanced instead of High Performance in Power Options.

💡 Persistent overheating usually means the thermal paste needs replacing — a 30-minute repair job.

On Android:

1. Check your Gallery → Recycle Bin / Recently Deleted folder first.

2. Check Google Photos → Library → Trash (items stay 60 days).

3. For SD card recovery, use DiskDigger app (free on Play Store).

On Windows:

4. Check Recycle Bin on Desktop first.

5. Use Recuva (free) to scan your drive for recoverable files.

⚠ Stop using the device immediately after deletion — new data overwrites deleted files.
