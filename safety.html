🛡 Data Sanitization Guide

How to Safely Wipe Your Device Before Recycling

Smart Fix & Recycle Uganda · Updated January 2026 · 8 min read
⚠️
Don't recycle before reading this

A simple factory reset does not permanently erase your data. With freely available tools, anyone can recover photos, bank details, and passwords from a "reset" phone. Follow the steps below before handing over any device.

Every year, millions of Ugandans upgrade their phones and laptops — but few take the time to properly sanitize their old devices before disposal. Your device contains a digital fingerprint of your life: bank account details, mobile money PINs, personal photos, contact lists, and login credentials for apps like MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, and WhatsApp.

When these devices reach informal recycling centres or second-hand markets without proper data erasure, that personal information becomes accessible to anyone with basic recovery software. This guide will walk you through the correct process for every major device type.

📱 Android Phones (Tecno, Infinix, Samsung)

1
Back up your data
Go to Settings → Google → Backup → Back Up Now. Also manually copy photos to Google Photos or a USB drive.
2
Remove your SIM and SD card
Use a SIM ejector pin. The SIM card contains your contacts and network identity — always remove it before handover.
3
Sign out of all accounts
Settings → Accounts → Google → Remove Account. Do the same for Samsung Account, Facebook, and banking apps.
4
Encrypt the phone first
Settings → Security → Encryption → Encrypt Phone. This scrambles data before the wipe, making recovery nearly impossible.
5
Perform a Factory Reset
Settings → General Management → Reset → Factory Data Reset → Reset Device. Enter your PIN when prompted.

💻 Windows Laptops & PCs

1
Back up all important files
Copy Documents, Downloads, Desktop, and Pictures folders to an external drive or Google Drive.
2
Sign out of Microsoft & other accounts
Settings → Accounts → Your Info → Sign out. Also log out of Chrome, Firefox, and any saved passwords.
3
Use "Reset this PC" with full drive wipe
Settings → Update & Security → Recovery → Reset this PC → Remove Everything → Remove files and clean the drive. This option overwrites all data.
4
For older drives (HDD), use DBAN
Download DBAN (Darik's Boot and Nuke) on a USB drive and run a DoD 5220.22-M wipe for complete 7-pass erasure of mechanical hard drives.

❌ What NOT to Do

✗Don't just delete photos and files — deleted files are recoverable.
✗Don't rely on a simple factory reset alone — always encrypt first.
✗Don't physically destroy the device — this is dangerous and creates toxic waste.
✗Don't leave your SIM card in the device.
✗Don't hand over a device with active Mobile Money or banking apps still logged in.
